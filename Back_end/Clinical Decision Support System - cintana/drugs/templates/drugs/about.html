About CDSS -Drug Interaction

CDSS -Drug Interaction is an evidence-oriented platform for clinical decision support and drug research exploration. In this focused release, the system centers on molecular network visualization for drug interaction interpretation.

Note: this application is for informational use and initial screening. Clinical decisions must rely on professional judgement, treatment guidelines, patient context, and up-to-date references.

Current Scope

  • Molecular Network Map: interactive graph of drug relationships to targets, genes, food interactions, and neighboring drugs.
  • Comparative Matrix: side-by-side structure and target comparison for selected drugs.
  • Node Detail Panel: contextual information for each selected graph node.

Key Limitations

  • The system is not a substitute for physician or pharmacist clinical decision-making.
  • Coverage depends on available DrugBank records and curation quality.
  • Network visualizations are exploratory and should be interpreted with clinical context.
  • Absence of a displayed interaction is not proof of no clinical risk.

Quick Usage

1) Select Drugs

  1. Open Molecular Map from the top menu.
  2. Enter drug names or DrugBank IDs (up to 6).
  3. Click Visualize to build the network.

2) Interpret the Graph

  1. Use toggles to show or hide targets, genes, enzymes, carriers, transporters, food, and interactions.
  2. Click nodes to inspect details in the side panel.
  3. Double-click a drug node to add it into comparison (max 6).
